Hi, This is Dr. Avishek Guin.

His current research mainly revolves around utilization of strained intermediates in organic synthesis.

Research Interests


Arynes

Donor-Acceptor Cyclopropanes

Bicyclobutanes

He utilizes various intermediates, such as arynes, donor-acceptor cyclopropanes, and bicyclobutanes (BCBs), to synthesize organic molecules by harnessing their strain energy. Aryne and donor-acceptor cyclopropane are both highly strained intermediates, with aryne having a strain energy of 63 kcal/mol and cyclopropane having a strain energy of 27.5 kcal/mol. We employ the highly electrophilic aryne intermediate in various multicomponent coupling, cycloaddition, and rearrangement reactions, resulting in substituted arenes and diverse heterocycles. Additionally, we utilize donor-acceptor cyclopropane for 1,3-bisfunctionalization through insertion or multicomponent coupling reactions. We have recently developed an interest in studying the highly reactive BCB intermediate.
